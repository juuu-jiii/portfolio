TapRacing

Play the Game!

Fast Facts

Role: Sole Developer
Timeframe: 7 weeks
Engine: Unreal 4
Language: Blueprints

Concept and Design Goals

TapRacing is an isometric reinvention of the traditional, 3D, steering-based racer. Vehicles turn 90 degrees instantaneously in either direction and strafe sideways. The chase camera is locked to the same orientation throughout, so being able to differentiate right from left is important at all times! Gameplay focuses on fast-paced moments filled with manoeuvring obstacles and tackling technical sections of track, whilst racing against the clock to complete laps as quickly as possible.

When on the track, players will also need to avoid collisions. Grinding against a barrier will slow vehicles down, while driving straight into a wall will result in a crash and the vehicle being reset. Both of these cost players valuable time, so keen judgment and sharp reflexes are key.

Running the Gamut

I wanted to expose myself to the different tools at my disposal within Unreal. Everything in this project (except the car model) was implemented from scratch, using tools within the editor.

Snappy Controls and Handling Model

Fast-paced gameplay is a core design pillar of the game, so this gave rise to the simple, snappy, and "instantaneous" handling model. Instead of applying steering forces, vehicles travel in the direction they are facing and are rotated or moved laterally at the push of a button. Players can thus take corners quickly as needed, making success reflex-reliant.

Checkpoint System

Checkpoints are a series of triggers along the track. A CheckpointManager blueprint references each of these in an array. It ensures players traverse the track properly and visit each checkpoint in the specified order. This discourages cheating behaviours like corner cutting.

Players get reset to the location of the last checkpoint visited if they crash their vehicle. Checkpoints were primarily placed right before corners, since crashes typically take place when cornering.

Next Steps

AI Cars

Giving players something to race against besides the clock will add a new dimension to gameplay. Placing "feelers" on the front and sides of AI vehicles will give agents spatial awareness, and allow them to anticipate upcoming corners.

More Game Modes

Additional game modes beyond the currently available time attack mode will provide more ways to play. One such example is a "survival" mode, where vehicles speed up after each complete lap, and players must last as long as possible without crashing.

An Updated Handling Model

Making vehicles slow down slightly when cornering and strafing will add a layer of complexity to gameplay, as defending positions will then cost players time.
