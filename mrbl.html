mrbl.

Repo Play Game

A mashup of the action puzzle shooters Luxor, Bust-a-Move, and Breakout. Players must make trick shots using pinpoint timing and accuracy to clear marbles bouncing around an arena by shooting projectiles of matching colours at them.

Fast Facts

Role: Sole Developer
Timeframe: 7 weeks
Engine: Unity3D
Language: C#

  • Designed a system that ensures players are only given projectiles whose colours match marbles and other projectiles in the arena to prevent levels from dragging on unfairly if players are not given colours they need.
  • Added targets to further centre gameplay around precision, by rewarding the player with bonus points for making trick shots and hitting optional targets in harder-to-reach areas.
  • Created a system that slows marbles down as the arena fills up to prevent players from feeling overwhelmed and keep them focused on clearing marbles. As the arena empties, marbles start speeding up again to maintain a balance of difficulty.



  • Leveraged a dictionary to track quantities of marbles and projectiles in the arena, so that the player is only given projectiles whose colours match marbles and other projectiles in the arena.
    • The key is the colour, and the value is the count.
    • The count gets updated each time a marble or projectile is instantiated or destroyed.
    • When all instances of a colour have been cleared, the corresponding entry is removed from the dictionary altogether.
    • The colour of the next projectile the player shoots is selected at random from this dictionary.
  • Used coroutines to define time-based gameplay events, like iframe animation sequences when the player gets hit and the spawning sequence of marbles at the beginning of a wave.
  • Added line-of-sight aim guard using a line renderer to both help with player aim and communicate the colour of the next projectile being launched.



Encouraging Trick Shots

mrbl. is about precision and making trick shots. Besides targets, however, the player has little reason to play aggressively. To incentivise this, longer-range shots could be rewarded with more points.

Designing for Accessibility

mrbl.'s mechanics revolve around the use of colour, so accessible design is important. Symbols could be used to denote colours in-game. Alternatively, the colour scheme could be made configurable.

Pickups

Power-ups that fall from the top of the screen can add variety to the game. Power-downs could also be dropped occasionally to keep gameplay interesting, by giving the player something else to avoid.
