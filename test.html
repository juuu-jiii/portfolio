I'm a Game Design and Development student at RIT pursuing minors in Computer Science and Math. I'm especially interested in technical game design, as I have affinities for both designing interactive experiences and architecting them digitally in code!

My primary programming languages are C# and C/C++. I also know some JavaScript and Python. On top of that, I am familiar with both Unity and Unreal Engine.

mrbl.

mrbl. seeks to answer the question: what would happen if Luxor, Puzzle Bobble, and Brick Breaker met, shook hands, and immediately decided to join forces?

I designed a matching system that tracks the number of balls in the arena, such that only colours that exist are presented to the player. I also made the system manage ball speed based on quantity to prevent crowded levels from being too overwhelming.

Project Orca

True one-touch racing in isomorphic 3D! Tap and hold buttons to accelerate, brake, steer 90 degrees, and strafe sideways. The camera's locked, so timing, judgment, and reflexes are vital to avoid crashes and set fast laptimes!

I designed the movement system of this game with simplicity and snappiness in mind, and had to work around Unreal's physics to achieve this.

The Tunnels

Set in RIT's underground dormitory tunnel system, The Tunnels sees you take the role of a student trying to find their way back to their dorm after a long day of classes. The walk seems longer than usual, however, and it's eerily quiet. Did you go down the right path? Why are the lights flickering? Are you being followed?

I worked on level, sound, and environment design to ensure triggers and audio/visual cues were placed strategically to contribute to the creation of an unsettling atmosphere riddled with dread.
